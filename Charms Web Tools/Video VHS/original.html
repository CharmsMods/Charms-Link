About VHS Effects

VHS (Video Home System) was a popular analog recording videotape cassette format developed in the 1970s. The distinctive look of VHS tapes has become a popular aesthetic in modern media, often used to evoke nostalgia or create a "found footage" feel.

Key characteristics of the VHS look include:

  • Low Resolution: VHS tapes typically had around 240-480 lines of resolution, much lower than modern digital video.
  • Chromatic Aberration: Color channels (red, green, blue) would often separate, creating a color fringe effect.
  • Scanlines: Horizontal lines visible due to the CRT display technology used with VHS players.
  • Noise and Grain: Analog recording introduced visual noise and grain to the image.
  • Tracking Errors: Sometimes the tape would misalign, causing visual distortions at the top or bottom of the screen.
  • Color Bleeding: Colors would sometimes bleed or smear, especially in high-contrast areas.

This tool recreates these effects digitally to give your videos that authentic VHS aesthetic.
